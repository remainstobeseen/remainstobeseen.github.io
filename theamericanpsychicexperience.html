 I. The American Psychic Experience

   Movies which put on display the grotesque American conception of the world and of itself. This list includes movies that, on the one hand, are conscious of and intentionally presenting a crtique (e.g., Sorry to Bother You, Network). On the other hand are those films, which in their mission to soothe, entertain, and congratulate the American consciousness, capture (with savant-like clarity) something true about the uniquely confused, vain, and loathesome character of the American Oversoul (e.g., The Blind Side, God's Not Dead 2). We might call this latter category footage in order to distinguish it from the first. There are a lot of movies that manage to do a little bit of both, and in their projects of American self-examination reveal as much about the observer as the observed, i.e., blind grasps (e.g., Richard Jewell, Dances With Wolves). To some extent this is true of the first category as well; a frenchman once stumbled on the correct insight that there are no anti-war movies. As such, there are no American anti-American movies. Not one fully escapes the gravitational pull of condesencion, self-worship, and ecstatic malice.
   There are innumerable examples of non-USian or otherwise subaltern films that are in fact subversive and anti-American and remain purely in the first category, but I am not presently familiar enough with these cinematic worlds to really find my feet with them. I won't try to speak with any sort of intelligence about anything like that, and instead isolate myself exclsuively to the library of canonical American spectacularum, feature films. The variance in cultural ingestion and reaction to these movies isn't really my consideration except in cases where it contributes significantly to the definition of the picture I'm trying to throw light on in the particular case (e.g., Dead Poets Society, Into the Wild). The important point is that the degree to which the feature film was a part of the xylem and phloem of the American incentives for cultural production and ingestion makes it, for me at least (an extraordinarily mediated person), an ideal medium for preservation. Like a peat bog. I've arranged it chronologically, which actually makes it a little more like coring out a hole in the rock to find out how irradiated shit was in any given geological stratum.
